Egg Fried Rice

Description

Egg fried rice is the best way to use day old leftover rice. You can add any bits and bobs of veggies lying around in your fridge, a few sauces, 2-3 eggs and you've got yourself a seriously delicious dish!

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons Oil
  • 1 teaspoon Ginger finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon Garlic finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup Celery finely chopped/li>
  • 1/4 cup Spring onions finely chopped (white pale part)
  • 1/2 cup Green beans chopped
  • 1/2 cup Carrots chopped
  • 1 cup Mushrooms sliced
  • 2 1/2 cups White rice cooked leftover (approx Â¾ cup raw rice), refer to Note 1
  • 1 tablespoon Dark soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoon Rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon White pepper powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon Salt
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1/2 cup Spring onions chopped (green part)

Steps

  1. Heat a tablespoon of oil in a wok. Whisk the eggs and add them to the wok. Let the egg fry in the oil for 10 seconds and then scramble it, making sure not to brown it. Remove it from the wok.
  2. In the same wok, heat the remaining oil and add ginger, garlic, celery and the white part of spring onions. Stir fry the aromatics for a minute or two on high heat.
  3. Add beans, carrots and mushrooms and stir fry for another two minute, all the time making sure that its on high heat so that the veggies remain crunchy.
  4. Add cooked rice, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, white pepper powder, salt (if using). Stir fry again to make sure everything is mixed well.
  5. Remove from heat. Stir in 2 cups cheddar cheese and Parmesan. Stir in pasta; season with salt and pepper, to taste.
  6. Don't worry if the rice sticks a little to the bottom. Just scrape it off - those crunchy bits are yum! Add the scrambled eggs and green part of spring onions. Mix again and turn off the heat. Serve immediately.
